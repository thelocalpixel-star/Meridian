Retirement Readiness

Do Americans Decide Their Retirement Age/Date Anymore?

Restructuring, buyouts, AI, and severance can move a retirement date. The math should exist before employment uncertainty arrives.

By Robert Boeck · Retirement Roadmap Experts

A lot of high-income professionals I talk to are quietly worried about something they will not say out loud at work: they are not sure their retirement date is theirs anymore.

  • A restructuring.
  • A buyout offer.
  • AI moving up the organizational chart.
  • A severance package that lands before they were ready.

Here is what almost nobody connects: the moment your employment situation becomes uncertain is the worst possible time to start asking retirement planning questions—and the best possible time to have already answered them.

A hope is not a written plan

If your plan only exists in your head—“I will work until 65, then we will figure it out”—you do not have a plan. You have a hope.

  • A hope cannot tell you whether to take the severance and retire two years early.
  • A hope cannot tell you whether to convert part of your IRA in the gap year between your last paycheck and Social Security.
  • A hope cannot tell you how much guaranteed income you can lock in versus how much to keep at risk.

The math has to exist before you need it. Not after.
